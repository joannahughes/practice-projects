ABOUT SPEC

SPEC is a research and design collaborative interested in the myriad potentialities of an unknown future city, an alternative landscape not yet visible on the horizon. The city of the present, the city we currently inhabit as both conceptual terrain and material territory, is presented with a paradox–driven by both speculation and specification. Rampant market speculation, post-political utopian speculation, speculative realisms, speculative fictions: these and other gestures into the opaque future define contemporary urban life and culture. At the same time, techno-bureaucratic specification often denigrates the role of urbanists to implementers of econometrics, regulations, checklists, 'best practices.'

SPEC seeks to exploit these two paradigms, angling their arguments, constellating their desires, exploring their affordances and dismantling their mechanized prohibitions. SPEC explores the intersection of cultural landscapes, design policy, and the mechanisms for creating alternative future cities.

Founding Members
  • Samuel L. Friesema

    Professional Title

    With degrees in architecture and urban design, Friesema seeks to engage with the design and configuration of the built environment to enhance human flourishing in our cities and neighborhoods. His primary focus is on research into the interaction of architecture, policy, and infrastructure space, to construct development models that appropriately contribute to globalizing and mechanized urban form.

  • Michelle Kirby

    Professional Title

    A graduate of Parsons the New School for Design, her work is interdisciplinary, focused on creative coding and interaction design in the urban environment. Her work has been exhibited in New York, Dublin, Ireland and across Ohio.

    contact@contact.com

  • Jeffrey Kruth

    Professional Title

    An urbanist and educator at the Cleveland Urban Design Collaborative (CUDC), part of Kent State University’s College of Architecture and Environmental Design. Previously, he designed affordable housing in New Haven, CT and worked at the Yale Urban Design Workshop. His work on vacancy, infrastructure and urban policy can be found in CLOG and the journal, Cities and the Environment.

    contact@contact.com

  • Allison Schifani

    Professional Title

    Assistant Professor of Digital Humanities in the Department of Modern Languages and Literature at the University of Miami in Florida. She received her PhD from the University of California, Santa Barbara's Graduate Program in Comparative Literature. Her work on media technologies, art and the urban can be found in The Journal of Urban Cultural Studies and Media Fields.
