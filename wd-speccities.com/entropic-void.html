  • Genetic Engineering of Urban Spaces

Using experimental workflows in GIS, this project is informed by the proposition that geodemographic datasets inherent in existing GIS workflows are suspect, yet lively actors in creating and reifying existing contexts and patterns of development. With the onslaught of “Smart City” technologies and the collection of data pertaining to multiple aspects of urban life, this project seeks to redefine GIS spatial analysis tools to make present new systems of interaction for new forms of public life.

Through the manipulation of spatial and demographic datasets using TIN modeling in ArcGIS, new social cohesions are suggested to realize latent potentials between populations and space. This project is focused on a geography between Cleveland’s recent “Health Tech Corridor” and proposed “Opportunity Corridor.” This area is currently seen as having little economic potential, but through analysis is discovered to have rich social connections.

This work was completed with consultation from McLain Clutter of the University of Michigan, and with assistance from graduate students Katelyn Millius & Tyler Middendorf.
