Winner of the 2013 Activate Union Station Placemaking competition, Blah Blah Blob! was installed to enliven Chicago's landmark public plaza and station to create an unconventional cultural asset. A large bright, billowy, and inviting blob transforms the idea that public art is to be observed, and never touched. Suggesting what Umberto Eco describes as an “open work,” the blob recasts public art to expose it to the idea of a maximum possible “opening” of interpretation and interaction through an intentionally unfinished nature and form.

Encouraging each passing Union Station commuter, employee, tourist, and wanderer to touch and occupy the soft object, its meaning changed throughout its week long installation. Yoga classes, happy hour, lounging, and children's running suggests that the city and its space are here for its inhabitants to use and interpret.

This work was completed as part of the Cleveland Urban Design Collaborative.
